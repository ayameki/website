President Crawford Fights for Delta Tau Delta to Return on Campus

Posted: 6:11 PM, Feb 10, 2020
Updated: 2020-02-11 18:18:52-04
By: WCPO staff
Creative Commons

OXFORD, Ohio —  A university in Ohio is reconsidering a suspended fraternity over what a student said was a "brutal and deplorable" report of hazing. Nearly 20 members of Miami University's Delta Tau Delta fraternity are facing criminal charges in connection with a hazing last spring. The indicted fraternity members face a mix of low-level felony or misdemeanor charges. If convicted, they face maximum penalties ranging from probation to up to 18 months in jail.

After news leaked about possible charges, University President Gregory Crawford starts a campaign in hopes to protect his students and bring back Delta Tau Delta. President Crawford called an emergent press conference on January 27, 2020, proposing an idea to fight for the men to be a recognized chapter on campus again.
